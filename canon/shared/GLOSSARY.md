# HDL Canon --- Glossary

This glossary defines key terminology used throughout the HDL World
Engine canon.

The purpose of this document is to ensure that terms are used
consistently across all worlds, rules, entity definitions, and future
documentation.

If a term is used in canon documents, its meaning should align with the
definitions here.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# HDL World

The **HDL World** is the central environment of the HDL universe.

It is a safe, interior world that connects all blockchain ecosystems.\
Users begin here and may travel to external blockchain worlds through
portals.

The HDL World contains:

• the helmet interior\
• OD (the guiding intelligence)\
• the Twelve Disciples\
• the user's wallet identity\
• navigation portals

It functions as the **home base and anchor world**.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Helmet

The **Helmet** is the structural environment through which the HDL World
is experienced.

Users perceive the universe from inside the helmet.

The helmet provides:

• spatial boundaries\
• visual framing of external worlds\
• a controlled environment for interaction

External worlds are visible through the helmet but cannot be physically
entered without portals.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Horizon

The **Horizon** is the visible boundary between the interior HDL World
and the vast external universe.

Through the horizon, users may see distant blockchain worlds or
structures in space.

The horizon reinforces that the HDL World is contained while the broader
universe exists beyond it.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Portal

A **Portal** is the mechanism used to travel between worlds.

Portals:

• originate in the HDL World\
• lead to external blockchain worlds\
• allow reversible travel\
• require intentional user interaction

Direct travel between external worlds does not occur.\
All travel routes through HDL.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# External World

An **External World** is a blockchain ecosystem represented as its own
environment.

Examples include:

• Bitcoin World\
• Ethereum World\
• XRP World\
• Solana World

External worlds contain their own entities, symbolism, and mechanics.

They exist outside the HDL World but remain accessible through portals.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Anchor World

The **Anchor World** is the permanent safe location of the universe.

In the HDL universe, the HDL World is the anchor world.

Regardless of where users travel, they can always return to this
location.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# OD

**OD** is the guiding intelligence of the HDL universe.

OD resides in the HDL World and helps maintain coherence, orientation,
and navigation.

OD's role includes:

• welcoming users\
• guiding exploration\
• maintaining world continuity\
• protecting the tone of the environment

OD does not override user agency but ensures the world behaves according
to canon.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Disciple

A **Disciple** is one of the Twelve Disciple aircraft operating within
the HDL World.

Disciples symbolize the expansion of the Web3 ecosystem and the movement
of users into HDL.

They:

• exist only in the HDL World\
• represent onboarding and exploration\
• do not belong to any specific blockchain ecosystem

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Blockchain World

A **Blockchain World** is an environment representing a specific
blockchain ecosystem.

Each blockchain world maintains its own visual language, entities, and
symbolism.

Examples include:

Bitcoin World: • Satoshi Nakamoto\
• Genesis Block constructs

Ethereum World: • smart contract structures\
• gas mechanics

These worlds remain independent from the HDL World.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Entity

An **Entity** is any defined character, object, or symbolic structure
that exists within a world.

Examples:

• OD\
• the Twelve Disciples\
• Satoshi Nakamoto\
• architectural constructs

Entities must follow the rules and doctrine of the world they inhabit.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Canon

**Canon** refers to the official doctrine governing the HDL universe.

Canon is defined only by the documents contained within the repository.

If external discussions or interpretations conflict with the repository:

**the repository canon prevails.**

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Closing Principle

The purpose of this glossary is to stabilize terminology as the HDL
universe expands.

Clear definitions prevent confusion and ensure that future worlds,
systems, and contributors interpret the universe consistently.
