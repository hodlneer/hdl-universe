# Disciples (HDL World Canon)

## Purpose

In the HDL canon, the Twelve Disciples represent the people and ideas that carried Web3 forward when it was still experimental. They are not characters you talk to or vehicles you can pilot. Instead, they are symbolic aircraft that live in the HDL White World and bring new users into the HDL universe. Each flight by a Disciple signifies another pioneer crossing the threshold into the world of decentralized finance and trust-less systems.

## Canonical Definition

### Exclusive to HDL World

The Disciples belong only to the HDL White World. They do not appear in Bitcoin, Ethereum or other blockchain-specific worlds. They operate under OD's authority and are considered canon objects.

### Symbolize Onboarding & Expansion

Every Disciple represents a path that brought Web3 from obscurity to mainstream adoption. When they take flight, they are carrying a new user (or group of users) into the HDL ecosystem. The twelve paths correspond to different early communities, protocols or philosophies that opened the door to the broader crypto world.

### Immutable & Finite

There are exactly twelve Disciples. This number never changes. They cannot be multiplied, traded or destroyed. Their designs and call signs are locked in canon; any representation that contradicts this is considered non-canonical.

### Not Agents or NPCs

Disciples are not agents and they cannot be portrayed as interactive characters. Agents (AI or simulation) must never impersonate a Disciple. Likewise, users should never perceive the Disciples as personal companions. They are high-level symbols under OD's protection.

### No Combat or Competition

Disciples are not warships or racers. They do not engage in battles, races or competitive challenges. Their sole purpose is to provide safe passage and to signify onboarding.

## Function and Behavior

### Onboarding Vehicles

When a new user enters the HDL universe, a Disciple carries them from the White World into the appropriate blockchain world. The flight is metaphorical in the web version and literal (as a visual sequence) in the 3D or VR implementation. The path taken hints at the user's initial interest (e.g., Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana, other protocols) but does not lock them into that world permanently.

### Represent Expansion

The Disciples each embody a particular discipline or contribution to Web3 (e.g., cryptography, decentralized finance, peer-to-peer exchange, on-chain art, open-source development, privacy, etc.). Their existence celebrates the pioneers who built these pillars. As HDL grows, new worlds may be added, but the twelve Disciples remain constant, reminding everyone of the roots of crypto adoption.

### Operate Under OD

The Orchestrating Daemon (OD) governs all portal and onboarding functions. It approves each Disciple's flight path and ensures that the rules of canon are upheld. If something violates the canon, OD can prevent a Disciple from taking flight and instead return a user to the White World.

### Non-Interactive for Users

Users do not control or customize Disciples. They can witness them, but they cannot pilot, trade or reconfigure them. The only interaction is choosing when to begin their journey into the HDL ecosystem.

## Relationship to Other Entities

### Users & Onboarding

A user's first moment in HDL is accompanied by a Disciple. This is meant to honor the history of Web3 pioneers and to welcome new participants. After onboarding, users explore worlds through portals and anchors; the Disciples return to the White World to await the next pioneer.

### Agents & NPCs

Agents are simulated participants in worlds (bots or AI constructs). They inhabit blockchain worlds and may help or hinder users. However, they never take the role of Disciples, nor do they override the Disciple doctrine.

### Entities & Portals

Portals are the gateways between worlds. They physically connect blockchain worlds (Bitcoin, Ethereum, etc.) and the White World. While portals handle transport between worlds, the Disciples handle onboarding - one cannot replace the other. Portals remain open at all times, but Disciples launch only when a new user needs them.

## Visual Design (Guidance)

### Aircraft Forms

Disciples are depicted as sleek, futuristic aircraft. Each design nods to a discipline (e.g., one may resemble a stylized ledger or a cryptographic key). Their silhouettes are immediately recognizable and consistent across all media.

### Color Language

The Disciples' livery includes accents that reference their discipline (e.g., privacy might use deep blues and purples; open-source might use greens and whites). Despite these differences, they share common HDL branding elements to tie them together.

### Motion & Presence

Disciples glide gracefully, with no sudden movements. Their flight conveys calm authority and trust. They generate shimmering contrails, hinting at the flow of data and value they represent.

### Iconography

In UI contexts, a Disciple may be represented by a simplified icon (e.g., a stylized wing or halo). The icon never changes form and is reserved exclusively for the Disciples.

## Canonical Constraints

### Twelve and Only Twelve

There will never be more or fewer Disciples. Each represents a unique discipline of Web3; adding another would fracture the canon.

### Immutable Properties

A Disciple's shape, name, call sign and meaning cannot be altered. They are sacred assets.

### Non-Ownership

No user, agent or entity can own a Disciple. They exist for the community and for onboarding. They cannot be purchased, transferred or collected.

### No Commodification

Disciples cannot be minted as NFTs or used as marketing collectibles. Doing so dilutes their symbolic power.

### Safety & Security

Disciples never endanger users. They operate in a safe environment and cannot collide or crash. They are vehicles of trust, not risk.

## Narrative Context

When decentralized technology was fragile and skeptics called it a scam, a few brave builders and believers paved the way. They wrote code, ran nodes, swapped tokens across multiple wallets, remembered seed phrases and withstood ridicule from friends, banks and governments. The Twelve Disciples of HDL honor these pioneers. They are not avatars but symbols - each one representing a discipline that made Web3 possible: cryptography, open-source collaboration, decentralized marketplaces, privacy tools, community governance, peer-to-peer protocols, immutability, open finance, on-chain art, interoperability, censorship resistance and self-sovereign identity. Every time a Disciple lifts off, it whispers the story of those pioneers and welcomes a new soul to a world where conviction matters more than capital.
